Dear Foreign Friends,

This is a travel guide about China.

What comes to mind when you think of China?

Is it the adorable giant pandas, or the breathtaking landscapes?

Is it the modern cities with a cyberpunk vibe, or the rich cultural heritage symbolized by “The man who does not reach the Great Wall is not a true man”?

Or perhaps it's the spicy hotpot, sweet candied hawthorns, golden crispy Beijing duck,or the auspicious dumplings?

China is a country that sparks endless imagination and satisfies boundless exploration desires. It is a land filled with warmth, friendliness, and continuous endeavors.

At this moment, we sincerely present to you this "China Travel Guide," hoping to invite >you to visit China, experience China, taste China, and through this guide, help you have a delightful,tailor-made travel experience.

What is China like in the eyes of others? What efforts have we made to make your journey more convenient? Which Chinese attractions are best suited for you?

Let's go！ 

Today, there is an increasing number of people getting interested in China.According to data from Google Travel Insight, from 2023 to 2024, there has been a significant increase in searches by overseas travelers for flights to China and accommodations. More and more foreign friends are setting their sights on "China," showing strong travel demand for the country.

Simultaneously, there is a rising trend of "China fever" on social media.

On the popular overseas short video platform TikTok, searching for "china travel" yields thousands of related videos showcasing China's landscapes, architecture, cuisine, and more.

To better understand foreign friends' impressions of China, we selected 28 short videos under the keyword "china travel" with views over a million and more than 500 comments. These videos display unique landscapes across China. We collected and analyzed the comments from these videos to gain insights.

In social media comments, netizens often use various emojis to express their genuine feelings about the video content. We extracted all emoji expressions from the comments for statistical analysis. The results show that these emojis mostly convey positive emotions towards Chinese landscapes. In the emoji ranking, heart and smiling face elements top the list, with 🥰, 😂, 😍 being the most frequently used emojis by foreign netizens in the comment section.

Additionally, surprised and awestruck emojis (e.g., 😳, 😲) also find a place, highlighting the amazement and wonder China's scenery evokes.

Data note: All words shown in the graph have a frequency of occurrence of over 300

Furthermore, we conducted a frequency analysis of English text in these comments. Among English comments, foreign netizens often use adjectives like "beautiful, amazing, cool" to praise Chinese landscapes. Words like "cyberpunk, future, dream" express admiration for China's urban landscapes' sophistication. In terms of mood, "wow" appears most frequently, and "go" is the most common verb used.

In everyone's eyes, China is a land of beauty and futurism combined. Its vastness and diversity are part of its allure. From one city to another, you can witness vastly different landscapes, savor diverse cuisines, and experience a variety of cultures. The multifaceted China fulfills your multiple travel fantasies.

We conducted keyword co-occurrence analysis focusing on high-frequency verbs such as "go," "visit," "love," "want," and their collocations in the comments. Phrases like "want to go," "need to go," "want to visit," and similar combinations reflect foreign netizens' high interest in China and their strong desire to travel. Many foreign friends have already added China to their "must-visit travel destinations" list in the comments section.

We believe that if you can come here in person, China will surely elicit a "wow" from you!

To facilitate the transition from "want to go" to "be here," China is actively joining the trend of "mutual travel" with foreign tourists, striving to make everyone's trip to China more convenient and comfortable.

Firstly, visiting China is becoming increasingly simple.

We tracked flight and route data from the flight search platform "VariFlight" regarding foreign flights to mainland China. In January 2023, during the first week after the complete lifting of pandemic restrictions, international flights to China officially resumed, with 195 routes from abroad to China and 355 flights.

Over the past year, the number of international passenger flights to China has steadily recovered and continues to optimize and expand from the original base. According to official statistics from the Civil Aviation Administration, by the end of 2023, regular international passenger flights to China had recovered to more than 60% of pre-pandemic levels, serving 67 countries, with flight volumes exceeding pre-pandemic levels in 25 countries like the UK, and new flights to countries like Kuwait and Luxembourg. Currently, these numbers are still growing, signifying China's active opening to the world.

VariFlight data shows that during the 2024 Spring Festival period until February, we operated a total of 5,861 flights on 784 routes. The 2024 Spring Festival saw a new peak in inbound tourism to China. According to calculations from the Cultural and Tourism Ministry's data center, China welcomed approximately 3.23 million inbound tourists during the recent 2024 Spring Festival holiday, close to the level seen in the same period in 2019. The China Tourism Academy's big data monitoring showed that on Chinese New Year's Eve alone, inbound tourists exceeded 600,000. The festive atmosphere attracted numerous foreign tourists to China, allowing them to experience the lively and peaceful Chinese New Year with the local people.

If the cumbersome visa application process has made you "hesitant" about visiting China, there's no need to worry. China is actively addressing visa-related concerns.

Currently, China offers various visa facilitation services for 183 countries, including visa exemption agreements, unilateral visa exemptions, group tourist visa exemptions, and more.

On January 28, 2024, in Bangkok, China and Thailand signed an agreement for mutual visa exemption for ordinary passport holders. The agreement officially took effect on March 1, expanding China's mutual visa exemption "friends circle" to 23 countries. Meanwhile, China continues to expand the scope of unilateral visa exemptions. On December 1, 2023, China announced unilateral visa exemptions for six countries, including Germany, Italy, and Malaysia. In March of this year, China announced visa exemptions for another six countries, including Switzerland, Ireland, and Hungary. After the policy announcements, there was a significant increase in Google Travel demand indices for corresponding countries, indicating a surge in mutual travel efforts between China and the world.

 Hainan, Guilin, and other popular tourist destinations have also reinstated exclusive visa-free policies, welcoming foreign tourists with the utmost enthusiasm.

In addition, the national government continues to streamline visa processing procedures through an "all-in-one visa processing" approach. According to data from various Chinese embassies worldwide, citizens from 165 countries can now enjoy varying degrees of visa processing convenience, including exemptions from fingerprinting, visa processing without appointments, and gradual reductions in visa processing fees for some countries.

To facilitate tourists in quickly understanding the visa conveniences available to them from their respective countries, we have developed a query system. You can select your country of residence and perform a one-click query to plan your "China trip" according to the latest information, tailored to your needs.

Simultaneously, our inbound tourism services are continually improving. In September 2023, the State Council General Office issued "Several Measures to Unleash Tourism Consumption Potential and Promote High-Quality Tourism Development," emphasizing the enhancement of inbound tourism services. In the future, accommodations registration for foreign tourists in China will become more convenient, communication with service personnel in foreign languages will be smoother, and signage and guides at various locations will be clearer and more recognizable.

Addressing the common headache of "QR code payments" faced by many foreign tourists during travel, China is actively working to resolve this issue. With the widespread adoption of mobile payments in China since 2019, the Chinese government has been focusing on facilitating payment for inbound tourists and implementing solutions nationwide. Cities like Guangzhou and Sanya have tailored various mobile payment solutions for foreign tourists, including WeChat Pay, Alipay, digital RMB, UnionPay international wallets, and more. In November 2023, ahead of the China International Import Expo in Shanghai, over 36,000 overseas POS machines were installed in key locations such as businesses, cultural tourism venues, airports, and stations in Shanghai, providing full coverage for key merchants and meeting the card payment needs of international visitors.

In March, 2024, the State Council General Office issued the "Opinions on Further Optimizing Payment Services to Enhance Payment Convenience," outlining specific measures to improve payment services for inbound tourists, such as "large amount card payments, small amount QR code payments, and cash as a fallback." These measures consider both the daily payment habits of foreign tourists and align with the current consumption environment in China, with further implementation underway.

We aim to make consumption during travel "smooth and unobstructed" for foreign tourists: choose your desired accommodation, whether in luxury hotels or comfortable homestays; savor your favorite cuisine, whether in exquisite restaurants or street stalls; purchase souvenirs at your preferred locations, whether at famous landmarks or niche attractions...

In summary, there is no need to worry about these matters!

China is proving to the world through its efforts that as you step into China, China is also reaching out to you.

After addressing the question of "how to get there," we also hope to provide you with some suggestions and assistance on "where to go."

TripAdvisor is a global travel website that includes reviews and recommendations from travelers around the world. To gain a deeper understanding of the travel preferences and real experiences of foreign tourists in China, we crawled data from the "Things to do in China" section of TripAdvisor's website, including comments and ratings in foreign languages, for over 200 scenic spots, and conducted statistical analysis. If you plan to visit China, the genuine reviews from foreign tourists may serve as an excellent reference for you.

After analyzing the cities where these attractions are located, we found that the top five cities are Shanghai, Beijing, Xi'an, Guangzhou, and Chengdu. Moreover, more second and third-tier cities are also starting to attract the attention of foreign tourists.

Representing China's first-tier cities, Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou remain the top choices for foreign tourists visiting China due to their blend of international metropolitan vibes and historical significance, which strongly appeal to foreign visitors.

Two "cultural cities" — Xi'an and Chengdu — have also received high praise from foreign tourists for their rich historical and cultural heritage. They embody the "Oriental imagination" of foreign visitors — romantic and mysterious, reserved yet magnificent. In Xi'an, you can relive the grandeur of the Tang Dynasty in the Great Tang All Day Mall and witness the majesty of the Terracotta Warriors dating back over two millennia. In Chengdu, you can explore Kuanzhai Alley or Jinli Ancient Street, sit down for a pot of tea, and experience the folk customs of old Chengdu.

Based on the ratings and comments of these attractions, we have compiled a comprehensive ranking of these attractions' performance on TripAdvisor. Mutianyu Great Wall, Terracotta Warriors, Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding, and The Bund are among the most loved and highly recommended attractions by foreign tourists.

By extracting keywords from the comments about these attractions, we have summarized their unique features.

Mutianyu Great Wall:
It is said that "The man who does not reach the Great Wall is not a true man." As a world-renowned ancient structure, many tourists enjoy the challenge of visiting the Great Wall, with Mutianyu Great Wall being the most popular section. Besides hiking, there are many new ways to explore the Great Wall, such as the cable car and chairlift, which are highly popular. Being there, you will be surrounded by lush greenery and boundless views.

Terracotta Warriors:
Faced with three pits covering an area of over 20,000 square meters, compared to the diverse exploration methods at the Great Wall, visitors to the Terracotta Warriors prefer to follow guides and tour groups to ensure the best viewing experience in the vast area. Choosing a small Terracotta Warrior souvenir from the souvenir shop on your way out is also a popular choice for many tourists.

Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding:
As China's national treasure, the adorable giant pandas have numerous foreign fans. Whether they are sleeping, eating bamboo, or playing, the lives of these "panda stars" can always melt your heart. Additionally, here, the red pandas and baby pandas are also beloved by tourists.

The Bund:
Visitors to The Bund prefer the night time more, as the lights, night views, and the beautiful scenery along the river are typical features of Shanghai's The Bund. Here, you can take a leisurely stroll along the Huangpu River, enjoy a modern city-themed light show, and taking photos for your travel memories is an essential part of the experience.


However, the attractions presented on TripAdvisor still have certain limitations. They are mostly concentrated in popular cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. Yet, across the vast expanse of China's 9.6 million square kilometers, there are many more landscapes waiting to be discovered.

In China's highest-level scenic spots—5A scenic spots list, various distinctive classic attractions are widely distributed throughout China. Many provinces boast rich tourism resources, yet they are not well known to foreign tourists.

Here is a list of 5A scenic spots that are not on the TripAdvisor list but are worth visiting: Hulunbuir Grassland in Inner Mongolia, Changbai Mountain in Jilin, Gulangyu Island in Fujian, as well as the renowned "Three Famous Towers in Jiangnan" including Yueyang Tower in Hunan, Yellow Crane Tower in Hubei, and Tengwang Pavilion in Jiangxi. These are all excellent places worth visiting.

China has always been a "Land of Friendship."

During the Han Dynasty, China opened up two Silk Roads, connecting ancient lands across Asia, Europe, and Africa. The Silk Road spanned thousands of miles, serving as an important bridge for ancient China's trade and cultural exchanges with the outside world. It also symbolizes the friendship between the Chinese people and the world, known as the "Road of Friendship."

From the sound of camel bells to trains speeding by; from bustling cargo ships to the sky filled with flight paths. Crossing thousands of years of time and space, today, China has established formal diplomatic relations with over one hundred and eighty countries, opening its doors to people from all over the world. We are continually improving to provide the best experience for friends from afar, and what remains unchanged is our love and hospitality.

As an ancient Chinese saying goes: "When friends come from afar, isn't it a joy?"

Dear friends, we sincerely look forward to your visit here, to experience the unique and magical cultural charm of ancient China, to understand and discover the ever-changing development of modern China, to experience the warm and friendly hospitality of the Chinese people from the Land of Etiquette, and to unlock your amazing adventures in China!

China is always willing to embrace you!
